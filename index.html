Budapest

Budapest, Hungary’s capital, is bisected by the River Danube. Its 19th-century Chain Bridge connects the hilly Buda district with flat Pest. A funicular runs up Castle Hill to Buda’s Old Town, where the Budapest History Museum traces city life from Roman times onward. Trinity Square is home to 13th-century Matthias Church and the turrets of the Fishermen’s Bastion, which offer sweeping views.

Parliament

The Hungarian Parliament Building, also known as the Parliament of Budapest after its location, is the seat of the National Assembly of Hungary, a notable landmark of Hungary and a popular tourist destination in Budapest.

  • Address: Budapest, Kossuth Lajos tér 1-3, 1055 Hungary
  • Height: 96m
  • Opening Hours: 8am-6pm
  • Architectural Style: Gothic Revival architecture, Renaissance Revival architecture
  • Architect: Imre Steindl

Buda Castle

Buda Castle is the historical castle and palace complex of the Hungarian kings in Budapest. It was first completed in 1265, but the massive Baroque palace today occupying most of the site was built between 1749 and 1769.

  • Address: Budapest, Szent György tér 2, 1014 Hungary
  • Area: 4.73 km²
  • Opening Hours: 8am-6pm
  • Architectual Style: Middle Ages, Baroque, Baroque Revival architecture, Modern architecture
  • Designer: Miklós Ybl, Alajos Hauszmann, Jean-Nicolas Jadot de Ville-Issey
  • Did you know: Since the Bazaar and Royal Gardens are part of the UNESCO's World Heritage sites, along with Buda Castle and the Danube riverbanks, the monument had to be restored to its original state.
